The reading problem

AI chats are built for turns, not for long-form reading.

A useful conversation can become a substantial body of work. The chat interface still leaves it split across prompts and responses.

01

Fragmented context

Important information is spread across many prompts and responses.

02

Difficult revisiting

Long answers, research discussions, and tables are awkward to find again.

03

Limited overview

Scrolling through separate chat turns makes the document structure hard to see.

04

Copying friction

Moving everything into Word, Notion, or another editor interrupts the work.

Continuous Document Mode

See the document hidden inside the conversation.

ReadBooster brings the full exchange into one continuous document, so ideas stay connected and the conversation becomes easier to review as a whole.

  • Combines the full conversation into a continuous reading flow.
  • Preserves prompts so every response keeps its context.
  • Creates clear response sections and grouped outline navigation.
  • Moves beyond disconnected response-by-response reading.

Privacy by design

Your conversation stays in your browser.

ReadBooster accesses page content locally to build the document view. It does not automatically send your chat content to a ReadBooster server.

  • Conversation formatting happens locally in the browser.
  • Reading preferences may be stored locally on your device.
  • Feedback is sent only when you deliberately submit the Tally form.
  • You choose what text or screenshots you include with feedback.

FAQ

Questions about ReadBooster.

What does ReadBooster do?

ReadBooster transforms AI conversations into structured documents that are easier to read, navigate, review, and keep. It provides continuous and focused reading modes, an outline, clearer sections, table controls, and adaptable reading settings.

Which AI platforms are supported?

ChatGPT and Gemini are supported in the current release.

Does it send conversations to a server?

ReadBooster accesses the conversation page locally to render its document view. It does not automatically send chat content to a ReadBooster server. Feedback is separate and is submitted only when you choose to complete the Tally form.

Is it free?

The initial testing release is free. This is not a permanent pricing commitment, and later availability may change.

Does it modify the original AI conversation?

No. ReadBooster creates a separate reading presentation in the browser. Display choices such as section renaming apply to the ReadBooster view, not to the source conversation.

Can I print or save the document?

Yes. ReadBooster includes Print support, which can also be used with the browser’s Save as PDF option.

Are Mistral and Claude supported?

No, not in the current release. Mistral and Claude support is planned for later.
